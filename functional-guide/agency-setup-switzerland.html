Setting Up Your Agency — Switzerland (Zürich)

Admin how-to for a new agency. Example: an agency based in Zürich, Switzerland. The same steps apply to France and Italy (substitute the country); see § "What's not available yet" for current limits. Verified against the live app 2026-06-06 (v2.6.52). Related: functional-guide/deal-lifecycle.md, functional-guide/compliance.md.

What you'll set up

  1. Your agency (account + market) → 2. Your team → 3. Your workflows (deal pipelines) → 4. Your compliance rules → 5. Your first deal.

A useful mental model: you configure the agency once; individual deals then run on it automatically. You don't pick a country or a workflow per deal — the system applies the right ones based on how you set the agency up.

Step 1 — Create your agency

  1. Go to the signup page (/auth/signup) → Create Account: enter your Full Name, Email, and a Password (min 6 characters) → Create Account.
  2. You'll see "Check your email" — open the confirmation link sent to your address; clicking it confirms your account and signs you in automatically.
  3. On "Set Up Your Workspace" ("How will you be using HouseZoom?"), choose "I'm setting up for my agency" (creates an organization to invite agents and manage your team). The other option, "Just me for now," is a solo workspace.
  4. Fill the organization form: - Organization Name (e.g. your agency name) - Country → Switzerland - Region → select your canton (e.g. Zürich). - Timezone (e.g. Europe/Zurich) and Logo (optional) - → Create Organization
  5. You land in the admin app (/admin) as the agency Admin, on the Agency plan, with your country set.

What you get automatically: Swiss market defaults — currency CHF, phone code +41, m², and a canton-appropriate locale. You're the agency owner/admin, auto-approved.

You only do this once. Everything below is configured from inside the admin app.

Step 2 — Invite your team

Go to Team Members (/admin/members).

  • Invitations tab → invite agents by email.
  • Assign each member a role: Admin (full agency control), Manager (oversees deals), or Agent (own/participating deals only).
  • Optionally group members into a Team (e.g. "Zürich Sales") via the Teams page — useful for larger agencies.

Role determines visibility: agents see only their own and participating deals; managers and admins see the whole agency. Cross-agency access is always blocked.

Step 3 — Review your workflows (deal pipelines)

Go to Workflows (/admin/workflows).

  • You'll see the system templates by market. For Switzerland these include Switzerland Property Sale and Switzerland Property Rental (plus cantonal variants). These are the standard Swiss transaction pipelines — a handful of stages each (e.g. Lead qualification → Listing mandate → … → Land registry).
  • System templates are read-only. To make your own version, use Clone on a template, then edit the cloned copy's stages.

A workflow is just the pipeline — the stages a deal moves through. It is not the legal rules (those are Step 4, and they apply on top of stages).

Step 4 — Review your compliance rules

Go to Compliance Rules (/admin/compliance).

This is a 4-layer engine: Base, Country, Region, Company.

  • Base rules apply everywhere (e.g. "Deal close requires signed contract").
  • Country rules are your market's law — for you, the Switzerland corpus (Lex Koller, AML, notary/Beurkundung, etc.).
  • Region = canton-specific (see limits below).
  • Company = rules you add for your own agency.

What to do:

  • Set the filter to "Relevant to my org" to see the rules that apply to your Swiss agency.
  • Open any rule to read its source citation, severity, and whether it's blocking.
  • Use Add rule to author a company-layer rule specific to your agency (e.g. an internal policy on top of the law).

Every rule carries a source citation and a "verify against current law" disclaimer — HouseZoom provides decision support, not legal advice.

Step 5 — Create your first deal

Go to Deals → New Deal.

  • Choose Sale or Rental, give it a title, and link a property (e.g. a Zürich address).
  • Create it. The system automatically: - tags the deal to Switzerland (from the property, or your agency country), - attaches the matching federal Swiss pipeline (Sale or Rental) — no picker needed, - makes your Swiss compliance rules apply to the deal.

The deal-detail page shows the Swiss pipeline stages and a compliance status indicator.

What's not available yet (honest current state)

So you're not surprised — these are known gaps, tracked for delivery:

AreaCurrent stateTracked as
Canton-specific (region) rulesDeals apply Swiss federal compliance, but canton-level rules don't fire yet — the deal's canton isn't auto-derived from the property's postal code. Country-level coverage is active.BL-170
Custom (cloned) workflows on dealsYou can clone and edit a workflow, but deals currently auto-attach the standard federal template, not your clone. Customising is "author now, take effect later."(template↔deal wiring)
Compliance enforcementCompliance rules are evaluated and shown (status, severity, citations), but not yet enforced — nothing automatically blocks advancing a stage or closing a deal on an uncleared rule. Treat it as guidance for now.BL-172
Germany(Not relevant to a Swiss agency.) German compliance rules and Sale/Rental workflow pipelines both exist and are active — DE deals work end-to-end. The only DE gap is that DE templates can't be authored/cloned in-app yet (Create-Template dropdown).BL-171

None of these block day-to-day use: you can run Swiss deals end-to-end with country compliance active today. The above are refinements in progress.
